Welcome to Judith's Kitchen!

Judith's Kitchen is a market and cafe in West Newton, MA dedicated to providing fresh, delicious prepared foods from the finest locally-sourced ingredients. We offer prepared foods, artisinal baked goods, and a whole range of delectable, wholesome dishes for you and your family.

Judith's training was in the French tradition and her first job, at Hamersley's Bistro in the South End steeped her in the nuances of French Country cooking. She loves beautiful ingredients and combining tried and true methods with new ideas and twists to create fresh, innovative dishes that let the true flavor of the foods shine through.

Suppliers include Ace and Acme Organic Farm, Wiley Coyote's Fresh Eggs, Mary Quite Contrary's Fine Cheeses, and Moo-cow dairies.

Judith also prides herself on having a small but fine and eclectic selection of wine and beer to accompany her food. Most of the wines come from small single-grape producers who produce small batch wines by hand. Beer runs the gamut from small locally-brewed craft beer to larger brewer favorites like Modelo Negro

Come visit our beautiful, sunny store, conveniently located next to the West Newton Commuter Rail stop and the Express Bus stop.

We offer wine tastings and wine/food pairing events nearly every weekend. In addition, Judith offers cooking classes on various topics throughout the year.

Catering is also available for corporate and private parties and events. We are happy to consult with you about catering your next big event! We've catered events ranging in size from 10 to 1000, and everything from informal gatherings and private dinners, to large, formal, corporate events.

When you come to Judith's Kitchen for you food or catering needs, you will receive the best service, considerate attention, and the best food to be found!

Chef Judith Kalish

Judith Kalish, a local chef and West Newton resident, loves nothing more than preparing beautiful meals from fresh ingredients for friends and family.

A graduate of The New England Culinary Institute, she has been working in the Boston restaurant scene since the early 1990s. Judith worked as a cook at Hamersley’s Bistro for eight years and later served as the founding chef of Orleans in Somerville.

She has also served as manager of prepared foods for Whole Foods Market, and most recently as manager of prepared foods at Foodie’s Urban Market in the South End.
